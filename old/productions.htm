Misguided but well-meaning nuns, cows, angels and cookies take their part on the cosmic stage in this classic tale of milkman-meets-whore, set against the backdrop of the end of the world. Apocalypse: The Musical bounds through the surreal and the sublime, taking a pit-stop now and again to indulge in some lyrical gaiety which Tom Lehrer would be proud of: a veritable picnic of playfulness and punch. We'll treat you to some of the finest gospel-funk-tinged-musical-theatre-folk-parody-soul-rock music to grace a stage this year, whilst tackling the end of creation as we know it. With miracles, mayhem, misuse of an ironing-board and the best udder-choreography you may ever see, Apocalypse: The Musical is far too fun to miss.

Things aren't going so well for Joey. One of his old school friends has got him embroiled in stuffing the deceased pets of a rather serious looking lady with a gun, he's almost certainly tangled up in something illegal and his wife is sleeping with his accountant, who has delusions of being a hitman and taking Joey out of the picture. It seems the ideal time to check into HappyLand, the residential therapy camp that's taking the media by storm. Fliers, adverts, tv slots, chat shows- everyone seems to be talking about the wonder camp for the stressed and depressed. For just a modest monthly fee, your therapy will be personally supervised by a HappyLand counsellor, with bands of Smileys to make sure your every need is cared for, within the sanctuary of rolling fields and high-security perimeter fences. It's the perfect escape, the perfect solution to any problem, and a lot of people seem to be checking in... but do you know anyone who has checked out?
